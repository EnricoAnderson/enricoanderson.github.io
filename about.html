About

My Story

Since I was young, games have been my favorite thing and my passion. I started developing games in college, creating small projects and joining game jams. I later joined a program where we studied and developed games under industry experts. Two years working under their mentorship, followed by four years working with my own team, helped me understand what game development is really about, not just the technical side but also the business side.

I love every aspect of video games and enjoy exploring new ways to create fresh and exciting experiences for players. Game design is one of my greatest strengths, and my adaptability across different genres ensures that my work aligns with each project’s vision.

Key Skills

System Design

My experience in system design focuses on creating cohesive game mechanics that interact with one another while maintaining a strong player fantasy.

Having played hundreds of games throughout my life, I have developed the ability to come up with fresh mechanics and ideas that enhance both gameplay and the overall feel of a game.

Designing mechanics with player fantasy in mind is one of my core design philosophies. It allows me to create systems that are both engaging and enjoyable to play.

Player Experiences

Having led several games through to a commercial state has shown me that player experience and first impressions are some of the most important factors in making a game truly enjoyable.

As with system design, player fantasy and the overall experience built around it are key pillars in creating a game that feels cohesive and fun to play.

Creative Writing

Being a creative game director and designer naturally led me to writing. While ensuring that games are fun, I also recognize that a game’s story shapes how players perceive it. Creating an experience that stays with players beyond the screen is one of the key lessons I have learned.

Both story and narrative play an important role in making a game truly memorable.

Design Document

As a game designer working with a team of developers and artists, not everyone will immediately understand the intent behind a design or how it should be implemented. That is why creating design documentation is just as important as building the game itself.

Clear documentation helps keep the game coherent and ensures smooth collaboration across different teams with minimal miscommunication.

Game Balance

Game balance is one of the keys to creating a satisfying and rewarding gameplay experience. Over the years, I have learned that balancing challenge and reward is essential, while still staying true to the intended player experience.

Maintaining this balance ensures that the game remains engaging, fair, and enjoyable.

Leadership

Having led a small team of more than 10 people, I have learned how to communicate effectively with individuals from different backgrounds and situations.
